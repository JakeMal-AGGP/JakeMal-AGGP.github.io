I've been interested in the technical side of video games since I was a child. I would spend hours upon hours just walking around my favorite games, not necessarily playing them, but more just exploring them and thinking about how the game was made behind the scene, fascinated by the concept of being able to create a world where reality is under my control.

During elementary school, I acted upon my interests by messing around with child-friendly game development programs such as the drag-and-drop coding engine Scratch and later the more complicated ROBLOX engine which utilized written LUA. That being said, LUA was my first experience with written code, and ever since my first 'print("Hello World!")' in ROBLOX LUA my love for coding and game development has blossomed.

Later on in High School and College, I would start working with more professional engines such as Unity, GameMaker, and Monogame.
